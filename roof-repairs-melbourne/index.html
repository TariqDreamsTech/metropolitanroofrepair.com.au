Signs Your Roof May Have Leak Damage

When its pouring with rain and water is leaking inside, it’s pretty obvious you have a roof leak. But it’s extremely common for internal links to cause expensive damage – leaks which aren’t always easy to detect. It’s essential to detect these internal leaks early to minimise costly repairs. 

Watch for these common warning signs:

  • Water marks or streaks on walls and ceilings after rainfall.
  • Cracks or wear in roof valleys, which channel water away from the roof.
  • Loose or damaged flashing around vents, chimneys, or skylights.
  • Musty smells in upper floors or attics, indicating moisture build-up.
  • Overflowing gutters or pooling water, often caused by leaf debris in Melbourne’s leafier suburbs.

What factors influence the cost of roof repairs?

The cost of roof repairs in Victoria can vary depending on several factors:
  • Roof Leak Severity and Size:
    • Minor leaks: Small holes or a few cracked tiles typically cost $200 to $500.
    • Moderate leaks: Damaged sections or flashing repairs range from $500 to $2,000.
    • Major leaks: Extensive water damage, structural issues, or major repairs can exceed $5,000.
  • Roof Type and Materials:
    • Metal roofs (Colorbond): Repairs can range from $4,500 to $7,200 for significant leaks.
    • Terracotta tiles: Repairing or repointing can cost $6,300 or more for larger leaks.
    • Concrete tiles: Costs are similar to terracotta tiles.
  • Accessibility and Complexity:
    • Steep roofs or hard-to-reach areas increase labour and safety costs.
    • Complex structures require specialised skills, adding to the overall expense.
  • Additional Costs:
    • Labour: Professional experience and company rates affect pricing.
    • Materials: High-quality materials typically cost more.
    • Permits and Inspections: These may be required for certain repairs.
