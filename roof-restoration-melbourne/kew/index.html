Frequently Asked Questions

How much does roof restoration cost?

The cost of roof restoration varies depending on the size and condition of your roof, the materials used, and the complexity of the job. Generally, roof restoration can range from $3,000 to $12,000 for an average-sized home. For a more accurate estimate, we can provide you an obligation free inspection and consultation.

How do I know if my roof needs restoration or just repairs?

To know if your roof needs restoration or repairs, you need to assess the extent of the damage and the age of the roof. Here’s how you can tell:

  1. Cracked or Curling Roof Tiles: If your roof tiles are cracked, broken or curling, it’s a clear sign that the tiles are deteriorating and may be allowing water penetration. These can start as minor repairs but can escalate to full restoration if left unattended.

  2. Leaks or Water Damage: Leaks inside your home, especially after heavy rain, is a major red flag. If the leaks are frequent or getting worse, it’s a sign that the roof is no longer doing its job. A roof restoration will fix these issues, including repairing damaged components like tiles, flashing and ridge caps to stop the leaks.

  3. Visible Moss, Lichen or Algae Growth: Excessive moss, lichen or algae on your roof may mean trapped moisture which can cause tiles and roofing materials to deteriorate. While this can be cleaned off during restoration, it’s a sign that the roof’s protective coating is wearing out and the roof may need more than just cleaning.

  4. Deteriorating Ridge Capping: If the ridge caps (the tiles or materials at the peak of your roof) are cracked, loose or missing, it can allow water to seep into your home. Repointing and re-bedding the ridge capping during restoration will prevent further damage.

  5. Reduced Energy Efficiency: If your energy bills are increasing, your roof may not be providing adequate insulation. Older, damaged roofs can let heat escape in winter and let heat in during summer, reducing energy efficiency. A roof restoration with proper insulation and ventilation improvements will restore your roof’s thermal efficiency.

  6. Roof Age: Most roofs have a lifespan of 15-20 years depending on the material used. If your roof is nearing that age and showing signs of wear, it may be time for restoration. Even if the damage is not immediately visible, older roofs tend to suffer from accumulated wear and tear that can be more costly to repair than restoring the whole roof.7. Multiple Issues: If you see multiple issues on your roof, like leaks, cracking and deterioration of various components, restoration is usually a more cost effective long term solution than continuous small repairs. A restoration will address all the underlying problems at once and extend the life of the roof and reduce the need for future repairs.

In short, if your roof is showing signs of age, damage or efficiency loss, a full restoration will fix the problem for good. For minor issues, roof repairs may be enough. We’ll help you determine the best course of action for your home.

What's the difference between roof repair restoration and repair?

Roof restoration goes beyond just fixing leaks. It’s a comprehensive overhaul that addresses existing issues such as damaged tiles or failing mortar.

Roof restorations add preventative measures such as waterproof membranes, improved ventilation, and rejuvenate your roof with repointing and painting.

Restoration extends your roof’s lifespan, improves its performance, and enhances your home’s curb appeal and value.

What does roof restoration include?

A roof restoration from Metropolitan Roof Repairs is a comprehensive process designed to bring your roof back to its original condition and extend its lifespan. The restoration process typically involves:

  1. Thorough Roof Inspection: A detailed assessment of the roof’s current condition, identifying issues such as leaks, worn-out tiles, cracked ridge capping, damaged flashings, and deteriorating sealants.
  2. Repairs: Addressing any issues found during the inspection. This can include replacing broken or cracked tiles, fixing leaks, and repairing damaged ridge capping and flashings. Any structural issues, such as loose battens or sagging areas, will also be addressed.
  3. Repointing and Re-bedding: Ridge caps and cappings are repointed with high-quality mortar to ensure they are securely fixed in place, preventing water from entering. If necessary, the bedding (mortar base) under the ridge caps will also be replaced.
  4. Cleaning: A high-pressure wash to remove dirt, moss, and debris, restoring the roof’s appearance. This also helps improve the roof’s functionality by clearing drainage channels.
  5. Sealing and Waterproofing: After cleaning, a quality roof sealant or waterproofing treatment is applied to protect against leaks and further damage. This helps the roof resist the harsh Australian climate and prevents water penetration.
  6. Sarking Installation (if necessary): In cases where the existing sarking is inadequate or damaged, a new layer is installed to provide an extra line of defence against water ingress.
  7. Improved Ventilation: Ensuring proper airflow is crucial for roof longevity. The installation of additional vents or improvements to existing ventilation systems helps regulate temperature, prevent condensation, and reduce the risk of mold and mildew growth.
  8. Repainting: A fresh coat of high-quality roof paint is applied to enhance the roof’s appearance and protect against the elements. This step also improves energy efficiency by reflecting heat and reducing the roof’s overall temperature.
  9. Gutter and Downpipe Check: The gutters and downpipes are cleaned and inspected to ensure proper water drainage, preventing water damage to the roof structure.

 

Each step in the restoration process is tailored to the specific needs of the roof, ensuring both its functionality and aesthetic appeal are restored.
